AI Outbound Automation System
Replaces manual prospecting, research, and email writing with a fully automated pipeline from raw data to booked meetings.

Reps were spending 60 to 70 percent of their day on tasks that were not selling. Leadership needed to scale outbound volume significantly without growing the team or the budget.

+
What I built

CRM sequences trigger automatically with no rep involvement at any stage. I owned the full build: data flow, API orchestration, prompt engineering, CRM integration, and infrastructure.

project_02
Automated Research and Email Drafting System
Saves 45 minutes of prep per contact by delivering a research brief and three email drafts directly inside the CRM the moment a rep starts a sequence.

Every time a rep added a contact to a sequence, they spent 30 to 45 minutes on research and email writing. This was the single largest time drain on the team and a direct drag on pipeline throughput.

+
What I built

Designed and implemented a real-time webhook integration between the CRM sequence trigger and an automation pipeline. When a contact enters any sequence, the system pulls CRM fields, scrapes the prospect's website, retrieves relevant account data, and combines everything with context from the AI knowledge base. The model returns a pre-call research brief and three sequenced email drafts, each with a different angle, written in the rep's voice. I built the CRM writeback logic to populate eight custom fields per contact. End-to-end from trigger to populated CRM: under 60 seconds.

Tools used
Apollo.io (webhooks + custom fields) n8n Claude API Firecrawl Supabase RAG Public APIs JSON schema output
Technical ownership

Designed the JSON output schema for structured LLM responses, engineered prompts for consistent voice across the team, and built fallback logic for incomplete enrichment data. Writeback architecture was designed to fit directly into the existing sales workflow.

project_03
Prospect Scoring and List Intelligence Pipeline
Converts raw, unstructured account data into a scored, ranked, CRM-ready prospect list with no manual sorting required.

Raw public databases required significant interpretation before accounts could be used for outbound. Reps were manually sorting through hundreds of records, which slowed list-building and introduced inconsistency across the team.

+
What I built

Designed and deployed a batch processing pipeline that ingests raw account data from CSV uploads, public APIs, and CRM exports. It applies a custom AI classification taxonomy with 8 distinct categories, scores each account against a weighted ICP rubric co-developed with sales leadership, and outputs a fully enriched file with decision-makers ranked by role and seniority. Accounts below revenue or structure thresholds are flagged and excluded automatically. The contact discovery layer queries multiple enrichment APIs, deduplicates results, and ranks contacts by department relevance, removing manual list curation entirely.

Tools used
Claude API (classification) n8n (batch orchestration) Apollo.io Hunter.io Public data APIs openpyxl (Excel output)
Collaboration and context

Classification taxonomy and ICP scoring weights were defined in collaboration with the Head of Sales. Output format was designed for direct CRM import, cutting list prep time from hours to minutes.

project_04
AI Sales Knowledge Base
Grounds every AI-generated output in the company's real messaging and positioning so the content is accurate, on-brand, and trusted by reps.

AI-generated outreach was too generic for reps to use. The models had no access to actual positioning, competitive angles, objection handling, or case studies, so the output was not being adopted.

+
What I built

Architected and implemented a retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) system using Supabase with the pgvector extension as the vector store, and Voyage AI for embeddings, selected for quality and cost efficiency. Structured the knowledge base into semantic document types including messaging frameworks, product differentiators, competitive positioning, objection responses, pricing rationale, vertical pain points, and reference case studies. Wrote a custom cosine similarity retrieval function and integrated the knowledge layer into every downstream AI pipeline as a context injection step. Also built a separate interface for rep use during live calls. This system is the foundation all other AI pipelines depend on.

Tools used
Supabase + pgvector Voyage AI (embeddings) Claude API n8n SQL (custom match fn)
Architecture decisions

Chose pgvector over dedicated vector databases like Pinecone to avoid infrastructure overhead and keep the stack composable. Built the knowledge base before scaling any outreach automation, because rep trust in the output depends on content specificity, not just model capability.

Business impact
Always-onBrand and voice consistency
1 sourceOf truth for all AI output
HighRep trust and adoption
project_05
Inbound Lead Triage and Routing System
Reads every inbound reply, classifies intent, and routes the lead to the right next step automatically, in under two minutes, with no rep involvement needed.

As outbound volume increased, inbound reply volume became a bottleneck on its own. Reps could not triage replies fast enough at scale, which led to slow response times, missed high-intent leads, and lost pipeline.

+
What I built

Designed and implemented an AI triage layer that monitors all inbound signals including email replies, form fills, meeting requests, and re-engagement triggers. Each signal passes through a structured classification prompt that outputs one of five intent categories: ready to buy, interested but needs nurturing, objecting, unsubscribe or DNC, or referral. Routing is automatic: high-intent leads go to the meeting scheduler, objections trigger a personalized follow-up sequence, and re-engagements re-enter the pipeline with full historical context. Classification uses structured JSON output, not free text, to keep routing logic deterministic. Intent categories and routing rules were co-designed with the Head of Sales.

Tools used
Claude API (classification) n8n Lemlist (sequence control) Revenue Hero (booking) Apollo.io (CRM update)
Collaboration and context

Intent categories and routing decisions were co-defined with the Head of Sales based on real reply patterns. The routing logic is a configurable decision tree so thresholds and categories can be updated without touching the core pipeline.

Business impact
Under 2 minInbound response time
AutomaticRouting and re-sequencing
0Leads missed in queue
// 02 · approach

How I think about this work

The biggest bottleneck in most sales teams is not skill. It is time. Specifically, the hours that capable people spend on work that a well-designed system could handle. Every project here was built around that idea: identify what the system should own, automate it cleanly, and redirect the rep's energy toward conversations that actually require a human.

⚙️
Reps should only handle what requires judgment

If a task can be defined with clear rules and consistent data, it should not be on a rep's list. Research, email drafting, data entry, and lead triage all fall into that category.

🧠
AI output needs real context to be trusted

Generic AI content does not get used. I built the sales knowledge system before scaling any outreach automation, because rep adoption depends on specificity. The AI needs to know what the company actually says, not just how to write an email.

📐
Systems should compound, not duplicate

Each system here is independently functional but designed to feed the next. The classification engine feeds sequencing, which feeds the brief generator, which feeds the inbound router. The architecture scales without rebuilding from scratch.

📊
Every build decision ties back to a business outcome

The roadmap was built in close collaboration with sales leadership and tied directly to pipeline metrics needed ahead of a fundraising milestone. Technical choices were always evaluated by their effect on rep efficiency and revenue performance, not engineering preference.
