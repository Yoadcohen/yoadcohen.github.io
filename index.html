project_01
AI SDR
An autonomous AI SDR that turns the public IRS Business Master File into personalized, deliverability-verified email sequences. Runs 4x daily, zero human in the loop. One of the highest revenue-generating channels at Crowded since going live: ~$187K in pipeline and 19 opportunities booked to AE calendars (6 enterprise + 13 mid-market).

SDR teams spend 60–70% of their day on research, enrichment, and email drafting. That is the exact work most amenable to automation. The last generation of AI-SDR vendors prompt-engineered the email and called it done. That does not work in nonprofit outbound, where a misclassified org structure produces copy that reads alien to a compliance officer and gets spam-filed.

+
What I built

13 sub-workflows on self-hosted n8n, orchestrated by a central scheduler firing at 8/11/14/17 EDT. WF1 Lead Sourcing queries the 1.9M-row IRS BMF and dedupes against Salesforce customers via pg_trgm fuzzy name match. WF2 Enrichment fans out in parallel to CauseIQ, ProPublica 990 data, IRS group-exemption registry, and a self-hosted Firecrawl Docker stack for website scraping. WF3 Classification runs Claude Sonnet 4.6 with a cached 26-document knowledge base to produce a 10-level evidence hierarchy verdict and a 0–100 fit score. WF4 Contact Discovery combines Apollo + Firecrawl + Claude extraction, filtered through a granular role blocklist and board-member heuristics. WF5 Email Generation produces a per-contact 4-email sequence with structure-specific prompt variants, prompt-cached system messages, and Quality Gate validation. WF6 Sender runs hourly, per-persona mailbox, SMTP-verified at send time, with per-contact URL rewriting for downstream click attribution.

Claude Sonnet 4.6 n8n (self-hosted) Supabase Postgres 17 pg_trgm Apollo CauseIQ ProPublica Firecrawl (self-hosted) Reoon SMTP Gmail API IRS BMF (1.9M rows) Python (ETL + deploy)

project_02
AE Pre-Call Brief Generator (Inbound + Outbound)
When a prospect enters an Apollo sequence, a webhook fires. Around 45 seconds later the AE has a full pre-call brief and three ready-to-use email drafts written directly into Apollo custom fields. Live since March, used dozens of times per day by 3 AEs and the Director of Partnerships across both inbound and outbound motions. Prep time per contact dropped from 5–10 minutes to under 30 seconds, correlating with a ~80% lift in team-wide pipeline and an open-rate jump from ~5% to ~60% with a 25% click rate.

Before this system, every AE was spending 5–10 minutes per contact pulling data from CauseIQ, HubSpot, ProPublica, and each prospect's website by hand before sending an email or making a call. Multiply by dozens of prospects per week per rep. That was the single largest time drain in the GTM function and the biggest reason AE outreach was sitting at a ~5% open rate.

+
What I built

Two forked n8n Cloud workflows (outbound + inbound) that share 20 of 27 code nodes byte-for-byte. They diverge at the Brain prompt and the email prompt based on lead context. Pipeline: Apollo webhook → contact lookup → Supabase org_cache (30-day TTL) → CauseIQ match/profile/personnel → ProPublica 990 → Firecrawl deep scrape → Claude website parser → Compile Context (a 38KB node building a 15-section context blob) → Claude Brain (fit scoring + structure classification) → Claude Email V2 (three 400–650 character drafts with role-aware angles) → a 12-check Quality Gate → PATCH 10 Apollo custom fields → Supabase enrichment_log. The inbound variant adds a HubSpot Enrichment node pulling 34 visitor-tracking properties (full RB2B set) and threads a warmer tone through the email prompt, with explicit anti-tracking-admission rules so emails never sound like "we saw you on our website."

Tools used
Apollo webhooks n8n Cloud Claude (3 calls/lead) Supabase Firecrawl CauseIQ ProPublica HubSpot (34 properties) JSON schema output
Technical ownership

Prompt caching (anthropic-beta: prompt-caching-2024-07-31) on all 3 Claude calls with 6–8K-token cached system prompts. Structured outputs guarded by a 3-layer JSON parser (as-is → brace-balance repair → field-by-field regex recovery) so the pipeline survives truncated Claude responses. Hardcoded fallback emails ship with the workflow so AEs always have usable drafts even when the Claude Email call fails entirely. The downstream effect is the quieter win: AEs now come into every call with battle cards ready, know the right talking points, and sound informed and relatable instead of like another cold caller, which lifted conversation-to-demo conversion when prospects pick up the phone.

project_03
Nonprofit Classification & Evidence Hierarchy
A structured-output classification system that maps any 501(c) nonprofit to one of four organizational structure types using a 10-level evidence priority chain. Feeds every downstream email and fit-score decision in the pipeline.

Generic AI-SDR tools treat all nonprofits the same. In reality, Group Exemption orgs (many chapters under one IRS authorization), Same-EIN orgs (one EIN, many sub-units), Loose Affiliation networks, and Single Entity nonprofits each have completely different pain points, and completely different product fits. A misclassification doesn't just hurt conversion; it makes the email read wrong to a compliance professional, which is the single worst outcome in this vertical.

+
What I built

The classifier reads from five independent sources: the 1.9M-row IRS Business Master File (status code, GEN, affiliation code), a 3,418-row IRS group-exemption registry, CauseIQ (parent/child relationships, subordinate count, organizational type), ProPublica 990 (subordinate filings, group-return flags), and Firecrawl + Claude for structural website signals. Resolution rules are hardcoded into a 10-level priority chain with explicit anti-patterns. GEN=0000 is a placeholder, not a real group exemption. pp_num_subordinates=1 is a standalone org, not a GE parent. Chapter navigation alone without IRS confirmation is never GE. Output is a validated JSON with structure_type, confidence, evidence_chain, primary_pain, secondary_pain, recommended_products, deal_tier, and full classification reasoning.

Tools used
Claude Sonnet 4.6 Supabase Postgres pg_trgm IRS BMF (1.9M rows) IRS GE registry (3.4K rows) CauseIQ ProPublica Prompt caching Structured outputs
Architecture decisions

Every classification writes its full evidence chain back to Supabase so the reasoning is auditable after the fact. Hard-coded tie-breakers prevent known real-world misclassification bugs. These rules were reverse-engineered from production misclassifications and documented as regression cases with a dedicated golden-set eval.

project_04
RAG Knowledge Base for Grounded LLM Outputs
A Supabase + pgvector knowledge base that grounds every AI-generated email, pre-call brief, and classification in the company's actual positioning, case studies, and vertical-specific value props. Zero hallucinated case studies.

Out-of-the-box Claude is an excellent writer but has no idea what Crowded's products actually do, which case studies are real, or how to pitch a Group Exemption org differently from a foundation. Without grounding, outputs are confident-sounding nonsense that reps won't send.

+
What I built

Structured knowledge base in Supabase with 28 documents typed into seven categories: product, structure, vertical, case_study, playbook, website, competitor. Embeddings via Voyage AI. Custom SQL cosine-similarity match function (match_documents) for retrieval. Context injection is a mandatory step in every downstream pipeline. The Compile Context node blends retrieved docs with CauseIQ, ProPublica, IRS, and website signals before any Claude call. The Quality Gate includes a hallucination-blocking rule that validates every case study referenced in an email body against a known-good allowlist; any invented proof point hard-fails the gate.

Tools used
Supabase + pgvector Voyage AI embeddings Claude API n8n SQL (custom match fn) JSON schema
Architecture decisions

Chose pgvector over Pinecone or Weaviate deliberately. Supabase was already the system of record for every row in this pipeline (orgs, contacts, messages, tracking events), so keeping vectors in the same Postgres instance means RAG retrieval can JOIN against live pipeline state in one query. One less vendor, one less credential, one less failure surface.

1 sourceOf truth for every AI call

project_05
Reply Intelligence + Click Attribution
Reads every inbound email, classifies intent in English or Spanish, detects "left the org" and out-of-office messages separately from real replies, cancels pending drafts for departed contacts, and posts rich context to Slack. A parallel workflow captures every book-a-demo click and attributes it to the exact contact, message, and sequence step within 25 minutes.

Gmail's inbox is the default observability layer for most outbound motions, and it's a bad one. Auto-responders get counted as replies and inflate the numbers. Spanish-language out-of-office messages slip through English-only filters. When a contact leaves their org, the pipeline keeps sending to them for weeks. Click tracking is either bolted on via bit.ly (bad for deliverability) or never done at all.

+
What I built

WF7 Reply Handler polls Gmail every 15 minutes, detects OOO messages in English and Spanish, detects "left the org" in both subject and body, cancels pending drafts on LEFT_ORG match, and on real replies fetches the prior sent message (subject, body, step, sent_at) and writes rich metadata to ai_sdr_tracking. WF9 Slack Notifier polls every 10 minutes and renders reply, bounce, left-org, and click events as Slack cards with full context, so the AE sees the entire thread in one message. WF12 HubSpot Click Sync polls every 15 minutes, matches HubSpot page-view events to per-contact UTM parameters written into every CTA link by WF6 at send time, dedupes by HubSpot event ID, and writes click events to ai_sdr_tracking with persona/step/country metadata. WF11 Daily Health Report posts a prose operations report to Slack at 5 PM weekdays covering sends by persona and step, reply context, click details, bounce rate, and tomorrow's expected draft release.

Tools used
Gmail API n8n Regex intent classification HubSpot events v3 Supabase Postgres Slack webhooks cid/mid URL rewriting
Architecture decisions

Chose regex over an LLM classifier for reply intent. At 100 messages per poll cycle, an LLM call costs ~$0.50 per run and adds 3–5 seconds of latency. Regex pattern slips are fixed in 30 seconds; LLM misclassifications require prompt rewrites and regression testing. The escalation path is safe by default. Anything not matched by OOO or LEFT_ORG patterns routes to the AE as a real reply.

// 02 · approach

How I think about this work

The biggest bottleneck in most sales teams is not skill. It is time. Specifically, the hours that capable people spend on work that a well-designed system could handle. Every project here was built around that idea. Identify what the system should own, automate it cleanly, and redirect the rep's energy toward conversations that actually require a human. The four principles below are the ones I keep hitting in practice.

⚙️
One workflow, one responsibility

The orchestrator holds no business logic beyond batching and gating. WF1 sources, WF2 enriches, WF3 classifies. Every sub-workflow is independently deployable and independently debuggable. When WF3 misbehaves, I patch WF3 and nothing else changes.

🧠
Additive migrations only

Every production fix that touches the database is an ADD-only migration with constraint updates. No renames, no drops, no breaking changes on live data. Six production migrations shipped in April with zero downtime.

📐
Graceful degradation at every boundary

Every external API call has a fallback path. SMTP verifier offline? Send anyway. Firecrawl timeout? Classify from IRS + CauseIQ + ProPublica. Apollo returns stale data? Flag the contact as fallback, don't drop it. No single vendor can block the pipeline.

📊
Prompt caching everywhere Claude is called

anthropic-beta: prompt-caching-2024-07-31 with cache_control: ephemeral on system prompts. 6–8K-token knowledge bases cached across every classification call. Material cost reduction for repeated structure, and latency drops to single-digit seconds.
